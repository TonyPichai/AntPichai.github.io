Touchy Feely

Interactive Game Installation

Created with: Unreal Engine, Arduino and lots of hand made sensors and triggers

As creators, we wanted to challenge our preconceptions of how game controllers should be interacted with, and tried to mirror the way children inquisitively investigate the world with naïve restraint. Our playful approach led to the development of innovate haptic controls for immersive sensory experiences. Our final outcome involved a wide range of controllers which used capacitive touch, state triggers, piezo sensors and potentiometers to create controls, which create a dialogue between the participant and the interactive 3D world.

Video Sculpture

Created with: After Effects, MadMapper

Composed of scenes and archival footage from the locomotive industry, the layout of A2B accentuates allows the viewer to become immersed and contemplatively move within the film, evoking sense of the liminal space go to when in transportation

Narrative Machines

Interactive Video Installation

Created with: Processing, Arduino, RiTa(n-grams), Premier Pro, Repurposed Radio Controller

A playful visual exploration of Saussure’s semiotic theory and Wittgenstein’s language-game. The Narrative Machine lets users scroll through a large bank of scenes and select which scenes they want to contrast. Once selected the user then triggers a N-Gram procedure, producing a chunk of dialogue compiled from portions of the films. The results are vignettes that are both arresting and strange, sometimes familiar and sometimes confusing. Meaning is left to be explored by the viewer.

In the Eyes of Machines

Interactive Video Installation

Created with: Processing, Analog CCTV camera, Projection screen, MadMapper

A meditation on the Jungian concept of the ‘Self’ and the apparently contrary yet similar Buddhist ‘Non-Self’ in the context of computer vision and facial recognition technology. ‘In the eyes of Machine’ disrupts the computation of the facial recognition process by forcibly recycling the images of viewers again and again until the original faces are lost within the feedback and new ‘faces’ emerge detected out of visual the noise.

Stroboscope Sketch

Audio Visual Sketch

Created with: Processing, FFT beat matching

Based on a stroboscope’s shutter viewing mechanism. This is a procedurally generated film, where video frames are fired across the screen at rates of 100’s per second and jump cuts are made responding to FFT beat matching. The visual outcome plays with the execution rate of the render pipeline and the refresh rate of the monitor, with the smallest tweaks to seed rates, various unexpected patterns of compositional repetition are created.
